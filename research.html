Broadly, my research interest lies in the theory of operator algebras. Focusing on Hilbert space operators often provides insights into the study of structure theory of von Neumann algebras. I am interested in exploring key equivalence relations such as similarity, quasi-similarity, unitary equivalence, and using these to address approximation problems in operator algebras.

In “A stronger form of Yamamoto’s theorem II—Spectral operators”, we extend Yamamoto’s asymptotic singular-value theorem from finite matrices to spectral operators on a Hilbert space, proving that the normalized power sequence <∣An∣{1/n}> converges in norm and identify its limit through the operator’s idempotent-valued spectral resolution. The result bridges finite-dimensional matrix theory and infinite-dimensional operator theory, complementing Haagerup–Schultz convergence results in type factors.
